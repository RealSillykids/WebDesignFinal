  • My First Videos

    2009-2010

    2009 was the year my mom introduced me to Photo Booth, a program for macOS that allowed you to take pictures and record videos with silly effects by utilizing the built-in webcam.

    After learning how to hit the record button, I recorded hundreds of short video clips featuring me and my brother, Liam. Some of them are very boring, while others are way too much. The majority of them consist of me hitting record, saying some random line, and stopping the recording. I have included four video clips above (click the icons to view them) to give you an idea of what they were like.

  • A Little More Advanced

    2011-2015

    By 2011, I fully understood how to use Photo Booth. This was also the year Liam and I started to create actual stories for our "shows." The characters were as simple as they got, with the most uncreative names imaginable. Despite that, those characters stuck around, and are still used to this day.

    In addition, 2011 was the year that my mom gave me her old laptop. This allowed my siblings and me to record videos anywhere in the house. We utilized the different areas of our house for different scenes, which made us feel like our shows were similar to an actual show you'd see on TV.

    2016-2020

    In 2016, our mom allowed Liam and me to create our own YouTube channels. At first, we uploaded whatever we wanted to our channels. We made food videos, gaming videos, random life videos, and more. Our primary focus was Roblox.

    As time went on, Liam stopped uploading to his channel, and I niched down and focused on a single topic, being Roblox. While Roblox videos were my favorite ones to make, I still wanted to create other videos as well, so I created separate channels for those different types of videos.

  • Niched Down + Better Editing

    2020-2021

    In 2020, I changed the way I edited my videos. I made things more fast-paced and learned how to add small entertaining edits. Some of these videos I am still proud of, while most I do not like anymore.

    Around this time, my school had a project called "Genius Hour," where we could pick our own project. I used this as an opportunity to experiment with video editing, and it allowed me to get a better understanding of how I want to edit videos in the future.

  • The Present

    2021-PRESENT

    In 2021, I once again completely changed the way I edit videos. I started adding custom captions to every video, which greatly helped boost audience retention. I also edited everything to be at a much faster pace, without being too fast for people to follow.

    I also started doing more videos with my friends. which helped me become more confident in front of the camera. This allowed for my videos to be filled with more emotion, and helped create stronger connections with my audience.
